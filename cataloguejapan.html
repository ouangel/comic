JIRO TANIGUCHI

FURARI

Furari could be loosely translated as aimlessly, bend in the wind or go with the flow. But our stroller this time leaves nothing to chance. This time, Jiro Taniguchi is inspired by an historical figure, Tadataka Ino (1745-1818), the unnamed protagonist strolls through the various districts of Edo, the ancient Tokyo, with its thousand little pleasures. Now retired from business he surveys, measures, draws and takes notes whilst giving free reign to his taste for simple poetry and his inexhaustible ability for wonder.

$20.89

THE TIMES OF BOTCHAN
(Vol. I-IV)

This is the fictionalized version of the life and times of Japanese author Natsume Soseki during an era of great change from the traditional Edo period into the modern Meiji period (1867-1912). Soseki is considered the Charles Dickens of Japan and his image even appeared on the 1000 yen note for two decades. He is best known for his novel Botchan, on whose times this book is based and the short I am a Cat which is integrated into these pages.

(per volume) $16.60

THE WALKING MAN

Whoever takes the time these days to climb a tree in bare feet? To stop and observe the comings and goings of the birds? To play in the puddles after the rain has gone? To return a shell to the sea? The Walking Man follows a modern day Japanese business man as he strolls at random through urban Japan -often silent, usually alone- with his vivid dreams that let time stand still.
